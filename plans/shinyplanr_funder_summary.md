# shinyplanr — Smarter Spatial Planning for Biodiversity, Climate and People

## The Challenge

Countries are racing to meet global biodiversity commitments — including protecting 30% of land and sea by 2030 under the [Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework](https://www.cbd.int/gbf). But planning *where* to act is complex, slow, and often gridlocked. Stakeholder processes stall. Technical tools are inaccessible. Expert-led workshops are expensive and time-consuming. Meanwhile, ecosystems cannot wait.

There is a fundamental disconnect between the spatial planning tools that scientists use and the decision-makers who urgently need them.

---

## What is shinyplanr?

**shinyplanr** is a free, open-source web application that puts world-class spatial planning directly into the hands of governments, NGOs, and communities — no coding, no specialist training required.

Built by researchers at The University of Queensland and powered by the [*prioritizr*](https://prioritizr.net) mathematical optimisation engine, shinyplanr delivers rigorous, transparent spatial plans in real time. Users set biodiversity targets, choose cost layers, and add constraints — and the app finds the most efficient protected area network that meets their goals, in seconds.

Crucially, shinyplanr is **generic and transferable**: a single codebase can be customised and deployed for any country, region, or jurisdiction — from the coral reefs of Fiji to the deep waters of the Weddell Sea — all without touching a line of code.

---

## What Makes shinyplanr Different?

**Real-time decisions.** Most scenarios run in seconds. Stakeholders explore, adjust, and iterate on the spot — without waiting days for expert analysis.

**Transparent and user-driven.** Users control targets, costs, and constraints themselves. More learning, more trust, faster buy-in from all parties.

**Fewer workshops, faster consensus.** Stakeholders do not need to agree upfront. They explore plans independently, reducing costly and time-consuming expert-led meetings.

**Climate-smart by design.** Planning can be steered towards climate refugia — areas that will retain biodiversity longer under future warming — making conservation investments resilient to climate change.

**Compare trade-offs side by side.** Two scenarios can be run simultaneously, with maps, charts, and summary statistics compared in parallel, so the consequences of different choices are immediately visible.

**Downloadable evidence.** Maps, spatial files, target-achievement charts, and comprehensive HTML reports can all be downloaded instantly for reports, policies, and stakeholder presentations.

**Scalable across any geography.** Deployed in tiny island states such as Kosrae (Federated States of Micronesia, 195 km²) and vast ocean regions such as the Weddell Sea, shinyplanr adapts to any scale, data type, and planning question.

## A Broader Vision: Science Meets Stakeholders

shinyplanr sits at the heart of a broader philosophy: cutting-edge science is only as powerful as its ability to reach the people who need it. Our team has a proven track record of translating complex, data-heavy research into interactive, accessible tools — and shinyplanr is the flagship of that approach.

Alongside shinyplanr, we have built [**MiCO** (Migratory Connectivity in the Ocean)](https://spatialplanning.shinyapps.io/mico/), a Shiny app that gives fisheries managers, policymakers, and researchers instant access to synthesised tracking data for over 100 migratory marine species — from sea turtles and seabirds to whales and tuna — across ocean basins worldwide. MiCO bridges the gap between exponentially growing animal-tracking datasets and the international management frameworks that depend on them.

We have also developed [**The Climate Explorer**](https://spatialplanning.shinyapps.io/ClimateExplorer/), an interactive tool that lets planners visualise future ocean conditions under different emissions scenarios, identify climate refugia and hotspots, and understand how climate change will reshape marine ecosystems. This directly feeds into the next generation of climate-smart shinyplanr deployments, where conservation decisions are grounded in future projections, not just present-day data.

Together, these tools reflect a deliberate strategy: use world-class science to build open, transferable, web-based platforms that empower stakeholders — wherever they are, whatever their technical background — to make better decisions for nature.
